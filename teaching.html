Teaching

Psychophysiology, social neuroscience, and EEG methods at Freie Universität Berlin.

Philosophy

I use a student-centred approach built on reflective learning, peer instruction, and hands-on demonstrations. I treat the classroom like a research lab: students ask questions, form hypotheses, and learn by doing. Techniques include jigsaw puzzles for paper discussions, breakout room rotations, anonymous question waterfalls, Mentimeter polls, and mind-mapping exercises.

Supervision

I supervise BA and MSc theses in gaze perception, emotion processing, EEG methodology, and social exclusion paradigms. Carpentries Instructor Training (2022).
