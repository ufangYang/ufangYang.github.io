Research

How humans process social cues from faces, combining EEG, eye-tracking, and computational modeling.

My group investigates how humans decode social signals from faces. We combine EEG, eye-tracking, and computational modeling to understand the temporal dynamics of face perception, gaze processing, and social exclusion. We ask fundamental questions about which facial features drive emotion recognition, how direct versus averted gaze influences social processing, and what happens in the brain when we experience social exclusion.

Our research integrates cognitive neuroscience with open science practices. We prioritize transparency in methodology, contribute to community standards for EEG reporting, and participate in large-scale collaborative projects that test the robustness of our findings.

Gaze and Emotion Processing

How do gaze direction and emotional expression interact in face perception? I use EEG (ERP components like EPN, LPP) and eye-tracking to study the time course of these interactions.

Social Exclusion and Cyberball

The Cyberball paradigm simulates social exclusion in a controlled setting. I study how gaze cues modulate the experience of being excluded, with findings showing that gaze direction influences both self-reported distress and frontal EEG asymmetry.

EEG Methodology and Open Pipelines

Researcher degrees of freedom in EEG preprocessing can dramatically change results. I co-led EEGManyPipelines, where 33 teams independently analyzed the same dataset, and chair ARTEM-IS, an international EEG reporting standard.

Diagnostic Facial Features

Which facial features do we actually look at when categorizing emotions? Using eye-tracking with gaze-contingent techniques, I study the diagnostic features that drive emotion recognition.

Methods

Recording with EEG (BrainVision), eye-tracking, and co-registration of EEG and eye-tracking. Analysis through EEGLAB, MATLAB, Python, R, and JASP. Paradigms including Cyberball, emotion categorization, gaze cueing, and drift-diffusion modeling (DDM). Open science practices: BIDS-EEG formatting, preregistration (OSF), open data and materials.

Collaborations

I work across institutions and initiatives, including UCL London, INRIA France, Universität Würzburg, and multiple Big Team Science projects. I'm a member of SIPS, OHBM, Brainhack, Physiopy, FORRT, and the Chinese Open Science Network.
