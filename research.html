Research

A program of work.

How people perceive faces, direct attention in social contexts, and how reliably any of it can be measured with EEG and eye-tracking.

I · Perception

Face perception

How the brain extracts identity, emotion, and social cues from faces, and how diagnostic facial features drive overt attention and covert recognition. Combines ERP markers — N170, EPN, LPP — with fixation-based measures during brief and sustained viewing.

N170EPNfixation
II · Attention

Social attention

Gaze direction as a carrier of social signal. Work on direct versus averted gaze in interactive paradigms (including Cyberball-style exclusion) shows how gaze modulates early neural responses to inclusion and rejection.

gazeCyberballERP
III · Reproducibility

Analytic reproducibility

EEG and eye-tracking analyses have many defensible paths. Multi-lab, multi-team projects (EEGManyPipelines) quantify how much analytic variability shapes conclusions, and point toward preregistration, shared pipelines, and robust defaults.

many-analystspreregistrationopen data
IV · Methods

Methods

Dense-array EEG, mobile and lab-based eye-tracking, time-frequency analysis, preregistered designs, Bayesian and mixed-effects modeling, open data and code. Emphasis on defaults that generalize across labs.

EEGeye-trackingmixed modelsBayes

Collaborations

Active collaborations with labs working on social cognition, developmental neuroscience, and methods reform in psychophysiology. Inquiries welcome via email.
