Research

How humans process social cues from faces, combining EEG, eye-tracking, and computational modeling.

Gaze and Emotion Processing

How do gaze direction and emotional expression interact in face perception? Direct gaze signals approach motivation, but what happens when gaze and emotion send conflicting signals? I use EEG (ERP components like EPN, LPP) and eye-tracking to study the time course of these interactions.

Active: EmotionGaze (DDM) · GazeMatter (R2) · Emotion Gaze Cyberball

Social Exclusion and Cyberball

The Cyberball paradigm simulates social exclusion in a controlled setting. I study how gaze cues modulate the experience of being excluded. Key finding: Gaze direction during Cyberball influences both self-reported distress and frontal EEG asymmetry, suggesting approach-avoidance motivation shifts.

Yang, Fang & Niedeggen (2025), Scientific Reports

EEG Methodology and Open Pipelines

Researcher degrees of freedom in EEG preprocessing can dramatically change results. I co-led EEGManyPipelines, where 33 teams independently analyzed the same dataset, revealing substantial variability in outcomes. I also chair ARTEM-IS, an international EEG reporting standard.

Yang et al. (2024), JOCN · Chair: ARTEM-IS (INCF)

Diagnostic Facial Features

Which facial features do we actually look at when categorizing emotions? Using eye-tracking with gaze-contingent techniques, I study the diagnostic features that drive emotion recognition across different face types.

Yang & Gamer (2025), Scientific Reports

Methods

Recording with EEG (BrainVision), eye-tracking, and co-registration of EEG and eye-tracking.

Analysis through EEGLAB, MATLAB, Python, R, and JASP.

Paradigms including Cyberball, emotion categorization, gaze cueing, and drift-diffusion modeling (DDM).

Open science practices: BIDS-EEG formatting, preregistration (OSF), open data and materials.
